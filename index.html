Chromebook hardware

Poor man's MacBook Air version

  • FHD (1920 x 1080) 13.3in IPS display
  • Weighs 3lbs, 9 hours battery, stero speakers, webcam
  • Costs £270 (sale price £220)
  • Wireless, bluetooth, no ethernet
  • Intel N2840, 2-core 2580 MHz Bay Trail CPU.
  • 1 x USB2, 1 x USB3, HDMI, Audio
  • 4GB RAM, 16GB SSD, slot for SD card
  • No internal expansion - all soldered in

Chromebook software

  • GUI polished, consistent, Apple-ish.
  • Secure BIOS and boot.
  • Underneath is Linux - locked down.
  • Sign in using Google account password and id.
  • Default: anyone can do this, with snooping barred.
  • Pay extra for remote admin ($30 schools, $150 business).
  • Can placed in developer mode, which gives you root.
  • Results in powerwash first – wipes all user data.
  • Can then install another Linux as a guest OS.
  • I've done this, via crouton. Works well.

United States v. Microsoft Corp. (1998)

Court's findings of fact

As soon as Netscape released Navigator on December 15, 1994, the product began to enjoy dramatic acceptance by the public; shortly after its release, consumers were already using Navigator far more than any other browser product. This alarmed Microsoft, which feared that Navigator’s enthusiastic reception could embolden Netscape to develop Navigator into an alternative platform for applications development.

More findings of fact (1998–1999)

The rise of the Internet in turn has fueled the growth of server-based computing, middleware, and open-source software development. … Microsoft recognizes that new paradigms could arise to depreciate the value of selling PC operating systems.

… while consumers might one day turn to network computers, or Linux, or a combination of middleware and some other operating system, as an alternative to Windows, the fact remains that they are not doing so today. Nor are consumers likely to do so in appreciable numbers any time in the next few years.

Chromebook timeline

  • 1998: Google founded. (2004: IPO)
  • 1999: US v. Microsoft findings of fact
  • 2008: Chrome browser released
  • 2009: Chrome OS announced
  • 2010–2011: 60,000 prototype chromebooks distributed
  • 2011: Acer and Samsung produce chromebooks
  • 2011: Education scheme — $20 per month for 3 years
  • 2014: 4.8 million North America shipped.
  • 2014: US K-12 devices: 4.8m MS, 4.2m Apple, 3.9m Google
  • Worldwide shipped: 2014–5.7m, 2015–7.3m, 2015–8.9m

Oshkosh, Wisconsin, USA

  • Named after Chief Oshkosh (1795–1858).
  • Import centre for lumber industry.
  • Home of OshKosh B'Gosh is children's apparel.
  • Incorporated 1853 (population 2,800).
  • Population 66,000 in 2010 census.
  • 26,000 households, 25% with children under 18.
  • School district has 11,000 students in 22 schools.
  • U of Wisconsin-OshKosh has 14,000 students.

Chromebooks in Oshkosh

  • 2014 Referendum For Learning passed 7,324 v 5,004.
  • Extra $4 million per annum for 7 years.
  • Teachers got Chromebooks in 2014.
  • Over 300 teachers at 2015 EdCamp at UW-Oshkosh.
  • 3,000 high school students get one each (Sept 2015).
  • Public Library lends them for use in library.
  • Population of Oshkosh is 66,000 (in 2010).
  • Of these 12,000 under 18.
  • About 1 in 4 under 18's have Chromebook.
  • Roughly three times US under 18's average.
  • Younger grades to get them in 2016.

Do computers help at school?

The OECD report Students, Computers and Learning (204 pages, 15 September 2015) finds:

  • the reality in our schools lags considerably behind the promise of technology
  • technology is of little help in bridging the skills divide between advantaged and disadvantages students

The report also suggests that:

  • we have not yet become yet good enough at the kind of pedagogies that make the most of technology

Predictions for 2016-2020

Personal web server dongle

User cannot install software on managed Chromebook (except apps for Chrome). Anything else comes from web servers. Web servers need not be big. Can fit in pocket.

Recipe:

  • 1 x Raspberry Pi
  • 1 x USB Ethernet dongle
  • 1 x USB to micro-USB charger cable
  • 1 x world's shortest ethernet cable.
  • 1 x SD card loaded with lots of software.

Should work on Chromebook and Windows.
