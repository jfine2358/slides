  • Costs £270 (sale price £220)

  • Intel N2840, 2-core 2580 MHz Bay Trail CPU.
  • 1 x USB2, 1 x USB3, HDMI, Audio
  • 4GB RAM, 16GB SSD, slot for SD card
  • No internal expansion - all soldered in

United States v. Microsoft Corp. (1998)

Court's findings of fact

As soon as Netscape released Navigator on December 15, 1994, the product began to enjoy dramatic acceptance by the public; shortly after its release, consumers were already using Navigator far more than any other browser product. This alarmed Microsoft, which feared that Navigator’s enthusiastic reception could embolden Netscape to develop Navigator into an alternative platform for applications development.

More findings of fact (1998–1999)

The rise of the Internet in turn has fueled the growth of server-based computing, middleware, and open-source software development. … Microsoft recognizes that new paradigms could arise to depreciate the value of selling PC operating systems.

… while consumers might one day turn to network computers, or Linux, or a combination of middleware and some other operating system, as an alternative to Windows, the fact remains that they are not doing so today. Nor are consumers likely to do so in appreciable numbers any time in the next few years.

Chromebook timeline

  • 1998: Google founded. (2004: IPO)
  • 1999: US v. Microsoft findings of fact
  • 2008: Chrome browser released
  • 2009: Chrome OS announced
  • 2010–2011: 60,000 prototype chromebooks distributed
  • 2011: Acer and Samsung produce chromebooks
  • 2011: Education scheme — $20 per month for 3 years
  • 2014: 4.8 million North America shipped (5.7 worldwide).
  • 2014: US K-12 devices: 4.8m MS, 4.2m Apple, 3.9m Google
  • Worldwide shipped: 2014–5.7m, 2015–7.3m, 2015–8.9m

reveal.js enables you to create beautiful interactive slide decks using HTML. This presentation will show you examples of what it can do.

Use the Space key to navigate through all slides.

Slides

Not a coder? Not a problem. There's a fully-featured visual editor for authoring these, try it out at http://slides.com.

Point of View

Press ESC to enter the slide overview.

Hold down alt and click on any element to zoom in on it using zoom.js. Alt + click anywhere to zoom back out.

Touch Optimized

Presentations look great on touch devices, like mobile phones and tablets. Simply swipe through your slides.

Background Transitions

Global State

Set data-state="something" on a slide and "something" will be added as a class to the document element when the slide is open. This lets you apply broader style changes, like switching the page background.

State Events

Additionally custom events can be triggered on a per slide basis by binding to the data-state name. 


Reveal.addEventListener( 'customevent', function() {
	console.log( '"customevent" has fired' );
} );

Take a Moment

Press B or . on your keyboard to pause the presentation. This is helpful when you're on stage and want to take distracting slides off the screen.
